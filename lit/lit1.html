The story of sci-fi

Shelley- Frankernstein and Edgar Allan Poe - The Raven though, were not called "sci-fi" writers, but they introduced some elements of it.
Frankernstein- artificial being wants to find its place in the world, but is rejected by society.
The Raven- a guy instistingly hears sb talking to him, even seeing a talking raven.
H.G. Wells- introduction of extradiastials "The war of the worlds" and "The time machine"
At his time "sci-fi" term still did not exist, but some w.g. were already here

Golden era of sci-fi "Cold war"

John Campbell- Astounding and Hugo Gernsback- Amazing stories
Short stories dominated the branch, filled with adventures, cool protagonists, etc.
The new wave and experiments(1960-70s)
Philip K. Dick- "The minority report" and "Do androids dream of electric sheep?"
Cyberpunk and beyond(1980s)
Neal Stephenson- Snow clash, Willian Gibson- Mona Lisa overdrive and Paolo Bacigalupi(biopunk)- The windup girl
Up to present day: Solar punk
